## 3 Deal-Breaker Mistakes When Choosing a Jeans Supplier  

### Mistake #1: Prioritizing Per-Unit Price Over Total Cost  

A \$4/pair jeans sounds great. Until you factor in 12% defective units you can't sell, \$800 in customs holds due to improper paperwork, and two months of lost sales from delayed shipments. What we've seen repeatedly is that buyers who chase the lowest price end up paying more in the long run — sometimes double what they budgeted.

Ask suppliers for their defect replacement policy. That's the real test. Xintang Jeans covers return shipping for defective units over 2%, which is rare in the industry. Most suppliers won't even discuss this until something goes wrong.

![fabric quality control inspection textile worker checking denim](https://source.unsplash.com/random/1200x800/?fabric+quality+control+inspection+textile+worker+checking+denim)  

### Mistake #2: Ignoring Sample Quality Variations

One perfect sample means nothing. Order five different styles from the same supplier. We've caught factories sending perfect samples made by their top crew, then filling bulk orders with B-team work that looks completely different.

### Mistake #3: Skipping the Factory Video Walkthrough

Trading companies can fake almost anything. Except a live video tour showing your specific fabric on their cutting tables, your sample being stitched by their workers, and their washing station in operation. Why does this matter? Because most scams outsource washing, which is where color consistency breaks down.

## Wholesale Jeans Buyer FAQ  

**Q: How do I verify a supplier is a real factory?**

Demand a live video walkthrough. You want to see cutting tables with your fabric, workers stitching your sample, and their washing station. Scams often outsource washing, which is exactly where things fall apart on quality. If they hesitate or offer only photos, walk away.

**Q: What's the real cost difference between 12oz vs. 14oz denim?**

As of Q2 2026, 12oz runs \$0.85/yd wholesale while 14oz selvage hits \$2.10/yd. That looks expensive upfront. But selvage lasts 2-3x longer, which means your cost-per-wear is actually lower. Plus, customers who buy premium denim become repeat buyers at much higher rates — we're talking 40% vs. 15% for basic weight fabric.

**Q: Should I use Alibaba or go direct?**

Honestly? Both have risks. Alibaba offers some buyer protection, but you're still dealing with trading companies 70% of the time. Going direct to verified factories like [Xintang Jeans](https://xintangjeans.com) cuts out middleman markup, though you lose the platform safety net. What we recommend: start with one verified factory for your first three orders, then expand once you understand the process.
