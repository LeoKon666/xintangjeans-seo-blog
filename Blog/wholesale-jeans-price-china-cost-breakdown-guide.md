## How Chinese Jeans Factories Calculate Wholesale Pricing

Jeans pricing depends on six concrete factors that determine a supplier's bottom line. Understanding these helps you spot inflated quotes immediately.

### 1. Denim Fabric Quality
Standard 12oz denim runs $1.20-$1.80/yard (minimum 1,000 yard order). Premium 14oz selvedge jumps to $2.50-$4.00/yard. Stretch blends with 2% elastane add 15% to fabric cost. Organic or sustainable cotton? Expect a 20-30% markup.

Xintang mills produce 260 million yards annually. This gives local wholesalers like us better fabric pricing than factories sourcing from third-party mills - sometimes 8-12% cheaper for identical specifications.

### 3. Wash & Finish Complexity
Basic rinse adds $0.25/pair. Laser distressing? That's +$0.60-$1.20. Hand sanding and whiskers tack on $0.80. Eco washes using ozone or enzymes add $1.00.

What we've seen repeatedly is that brands overpay for washes their customers don't actually value at retail. Laser fades add 25% to cost but only command 10% higher retail prices in most markets.

## The Hidden Costs Most Buyers Forget

For first-time importers, the FOB price is just the beginning. These additional expenses catch buyers off guard every single time.

### 1. Shipping Charges
Sea freight runs $800-$1,200 per 40' container (holding 3,000-5,000 pairs). Air freight costs $4.50-$6.50/kg with 6-8 day delivery. Import duties vary wildly by country but expect 10-25% of product value.

### 2. Quality Control Expenses
Pre-shipment inspection costs $150-$300 per batch. Rejected units? Average defect rates hit 3-5%, and you're still paying for those pairs in most contracts.

### 3. Payment Fees
Letter of Credit carries 1-2% bank charges. Wire transfers cost $25-$50 for international transactions. These seem minor until you're moving $50,000+ orders.

To be fair, experienced buyers budget 30% above FOB for true landed costs on their first order, then adjust down as they refine specs and shipping methods.

## FAQ: China Wholesale Jeans Pricing

**Q: How much cheaper is wholesale from China vs. domestic suppliers?**
A: Even with shipping, Chinese jeans cost 40-60% less than US/EU wholesale. A $22 retail jean costs $7.50 FOB from Xintang versus $12.50 domestically. The math is straightforward.

**Q: What's the price difference between 300 vs. 3,000 piece orders?**
A: Volume discounts max out around 1,000 pieces. A 3,000-piece order might only be 8% cheaper than 1,000 pieces. Don't expect linear savings.

**Q: Do prices change seasonally?**
A: Absolutely. Expect 5-8% surcharges from August-October during peak production season. Best time to order? January-March when factories are hungry for orders after Chinese New Year.

**Q: How do I verify I'm getting quality matching the price?**
A: Always request fabric mill certificates, pre-production samples, and third-party inspection reports. Honestly, if a factory resists providing these, walk away - they're hiding something.
