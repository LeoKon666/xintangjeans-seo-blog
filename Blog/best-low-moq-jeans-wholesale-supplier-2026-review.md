## Step-by-Step: How to Order Low MOQ Wholesale Jeans

### Step 1: Define Your Requirements

Start by getting clear on styles needed - skinny, straight, baggy, whatever your market wants. Specify wash types like dark rinse, acid wash, or destroyed finishes. Figure out sizing since US/EU/Asian size charts differ wildly. Budget realistically: $500-$2,000 is typical for 30-50pc test orders.

### Step 2: Verify Supplier Credentials

For factory photos, you want real production floor shots, not stock images lifted from someone else's facility. Check for certificates like OEKO-TEX, BSCI, or ISO 9001. Understand that $30-$50 sample fees are normal - actually, they're a good sign because serious suppliers don't work for free.

### Step 3: Request Quotes Properly

Send style references using photos or links. Include your target price per piece upfront. Specify your desired delivery date. Detail any labeling or packaging needs you have beyond standard poly bags.

### Step 4: Compare These 5 Quote Elements

Look at FOB price versus CIF (FOB is cleaner for small orders because you control logistics). Payment terms should show 30% deposit as standard. Quality guarantee language matters - you need a clear defect replacement policy. Shipping method choice between air and sea changes everything for small orders. Documentation details on the commercial invoice can save or cost you money at customs.

### Step 5: Place Trial Order

Start with 30-50 pieces of 1-2 styles maximum. Inspect thoroughly upon arrival, checking every detail you care about. Then scale up with confidence based on what you learned.

## 5 Costly Mistakes When Ordering Low MOQ Jeans

**Assuming All MOQs Are Equal**

Some suppliers count "pieces" while others mean "pairs" - and yes, that confusion costs people real money. Xintang Jeans counts single units, so 30 jeans equals 30 pieces, period.

**Overlooking Fabric Composition**

Why does this matter? Because 98% cotton/2% elastane stretches completely differently than 100% cotton. Request fabric swatches before ordering, not after you've committed to hundreds of pieces.

**Ignoring Size Chart Differences**

Asian sizes run smaller than US/EU. Always get measurement specs in centimeters, not size names like "medium" that mean nothing across borders.

**Skipping Pre-Shipment Inspection**

Even 30-piece orders deserve QC checks - maybe especially 30-piece orders when you're testing a new supplier relationship. Budget $100-$150 for third-party inspection and sleep better.

**Forgetting Duty Calculations**

Jeans typically carry 16-20% import duty to US/EU markets. Factor this into your retail pricing from day one, or watch your margins evaporate when the bill arrives.
