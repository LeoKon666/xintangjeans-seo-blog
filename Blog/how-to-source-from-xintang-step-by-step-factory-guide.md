## Why Xintang Dominates Global Denim Wholesale

Xintang produces over 800 million denim garments every year. That's roughly 40% of all jeans made in China, packed into a small town in Guangdong province.

This kind of concentration creates advantages you won't find anywhere else.

### The Vertical Supply Chain Advantage

Within a 5km radius, you've got access to 3,000+ garment factories, 200+ washing plants, 50+ hardware suppliers, and 30+ denim mills. When everything's this close together, sampling happens in 3-5 days instead of 2+ weeks. Fabric costs drop 10-15% below market rates because mills compete for volume orders. For most brands, this speed and cost efficiency makes Xintang the only logical choice.

### How Factories Organize By Price Point

What we've seen repeatedly over thousands of orders is that Xintang's factories rarely operate across multiple price tiers. They build their entire operation around specific customer segments:

| Factory Tier | MOQ | Price Range | Best For |

Find the right tier for your brand, and you'll get consistent quality at predictable pricing.

## Step-by-Step Xintang Sourcing Process

### Step 1: Pre-Visit Preparation

Don't just show up. Get factory lists from trade organizations instead of relying only on Alibaba—those listings are 60% trading companies pretending to be manufacturers. Prepare detailed tech packs with stitch counts, wash types, and exact fabric specs. Schedule appointments. Walk-ins get quoted tourist prices.

### Step 2: Factory Verification

Real factories will show you their dyeing and washing facilities on-site, not just finished product showrooms. They'll have separate sampling and production lines, and they'll introduce you to their quality control staff without hesitation. Trading companies do none of this. They show you displays, can't explain technical processes, and push hard for immediate deposits.

Most first-time importers don't realize until their second order that they've been working with a middleman. By then, they've already paid 20-30% more than necessary.

### Step 3: Sample Development

Expect to pay $50-$150 per sample. That's standard. Turnaround should be 5-7 days—anything faster usually means they're rushing and cutting corners. Always require at least 2 rounds of samples before you approve anything for bulk production. Yes, this adds time. It also prevents disasters.

![fabric quality control inspection textile](unsplash:fabric+quality+control+inspection+textile)

### Step 4: Bulk Order Negotiation

Here's where experienced buyers separate themselves from amateurs. Key leverage points include payment terms (30% deposit beats 50% every time), shipment timing (longer lead times consistently get you better pricing), and packaging customization (simple polybags save 5-7% compared to custom boxes). Don't negotiate on just price—negotiate on the entire deal structure.

### Step 5: Quality Control

You need three inspections: pre-production for fabric and component verification, during production for first article inspection, and pre-shipment using AQL 2.5 standards. Skip any of these, and you're gambling with your entire order. The factories that stand out are usually the ones who welcome these checks instead of fighting them.

## 5 Costly Mistakes Buyers Make in Xintang

**1. Accepting vague contracts**  
Your contract should specify exact fabric composition, minimum stitches per inch, and color tolerance ranges. "Denim blue" means nothing. "Pantone 19-4045 TPX with +/- 0.5 Delta E tolerance" means something.

**2. Skipping chemical testing**  
REACH and SVHC compliance reports cost $200. Product recalls cost $10,000+. To be fair, most factories will claim their materials are compliant. Trust but verify.

**3. Underestimating shipping costs**  
Denim is heavy. Sea freight almost always beats air freight despite the longer transit time. Factor this into your landed cost calculations before you commit to an order.

**4. Ignoring washing variations**  
The same "light blue" wash can look completely different between factories. Request wash samples from every potential supplier, even if they claim to follow your exact specifications.

**5. Rushing quality checks**  
Dedicate at least one full day to pre-shipment inspection. Check random boxes throughout the shipment, not just the top layer. Factories know the tricks—you need to know them too.
