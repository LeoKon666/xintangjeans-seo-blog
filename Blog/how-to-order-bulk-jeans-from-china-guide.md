## Step-by-Step: Placing Orders from China (2026 Edition)

Follow this exact sequence. Skip steps at your own risk.

### Step 1: Calculate Your True Cost Per Unit

Most buyers only compare FOB prices. That's the whole point where things go wrong. Add these often-overlooked costs that can inflate your actual spend by 25-40%:

| Cost Component | Typical Range | Calculation Tip |
|---------------|--------------|-----------------|
| Fabric weight surcharge | $0.30-$1.20/piece | Heavier denim (14oz+) costs extra |
| Custom labeling | $0.15-$0.80/piece | Cheaper for woven vs. printed tags |
| Inspection fees | $150-$300/order | Third-party checks save thousands later |

Ask suppliers if their quote includes "standard accessories" - some exclude buttons, rivets, and zippers to appear cheaper. When you add those back in, suddenly the "bargain" factory costs more than competitors who quoted honestly upfront.

### Step 2: Verify Suppliers Beyond Alibaba

Three validation steps we require before sampling - because sending a $300 deposit to a scammer who disappears is a remarkably common rookie mistake:

**Video Facility Tour**: Legitimate factories will show dyeing, cutting, and finishing areas live within 24 hours. If they stall or only send stock photos, walk away.

**Production Capacity Test**: Ask "How many jeans can you ship per 40ft container?" The answer should be 3,500-5,000 pairs depending on weight and packaging. Vague responses like "depends on the order" signal they're probably a trading company, not an actual factory.

**Quality Documentation**: REACH/SGS reports for harmful substances. Yes, the paperwork seems tedious, but one failed customs inspection will cost you more than every test certificate combined.

## Common Bulk Order Mistakes (And How Xintang Jeans Helps Avoid Them)

### Mistake #3: Blindly Trusting Pre-Production Samples

What suppliers do: Hand-stitch perfect samples then mass produce with cheaper construction. The factories that get away with this trick rely on buyers not knowing enough about garment construction to spot the differences. When we deliver samples, we provide an identical sample plus a bulk production pair from the same batch - so you see exactly what 500 pieces will look like.

### Mistake #5: Accepting Terrible Payment Terms

First-time buyers often pay 100% upfront because they don't realize they have negotiating power. Even with a new supplier, you can push for split payments: 30% deposit, 50% after pre-shipment inspection, and 20% upon delivery. This structure protects you if quality doesn't match samples while still giving the factory enough cash flow to source materials.

Why does this matter? Because holding that final 20% gives you actual leverage if something goes wrong - which it will on roughly 1 in 8 orders based on industry averages.
