## Step-by-Step: Importing Jeans from China to USA

### Step 1: Verify Supplier Credentials
Check for OEKO-TEX® certification to avoid AZO dyes banned in the US. Confirm minimum 3 years exporting to US—ask for actual shipping records, not vague claims. Require video of their production line. Not just a showroom.

### Step 2: Negotiate MOQ and Pricing
Standard MOQ runs 300-500 pieces per style/color. Price benchmarks for basic skinny jeans fall between $8.50-$11.50, distressed styles cost $12-$16, and custom washes add $1.50-$3/unit. These numbers shift based on fabric sourcing, but they're your baseline.

### Step 3: Specify US Sizing
Provide exact measurement charts in inches. Don't assume "Asian L = US M" because that assumption kills margins through returns. Xintang Jeans keeps US size specs on file for all clients, which saves weeks of back-and-forth on every order.

### Step 4: Arrange Shipping
| Method | Cost | Time | Best For |
|--------|------|------|----------|
| Air Express | $5.50-$7/kg | 5-8 days | First samples, small orders |
| Sea LCL | $1.20-$1.80/kg | 30-35 days | Orders under 1,000 units |
| Sea FCL | $0.85-$1.10/kg | 28-32 days | 1,000+ units |

### Step 5: Prepare Customs Docs
Your supplier should provide a commercial invoice with correct HS code (6203.42 for cotton jeans), packing list with carton dimensions, and Certificate of Origin to reduce some duties. If they're asking you to figure this out, find a different supplier.

## 5 Costly Mistakes US Buyers Make

**Not Testing Wash Durability**  
Asian stone washes often fade faster than US consumers expect. Always request 5+ home wash test reports. The factories that stand out are usually the ones who do this automatically.

**Ignoring Labeling Laws**  
US law requires fiber content, country of origin, and care labels in English. Many Chinese suppliers charge extra for this. Budget for it upfront.

**Underestimating Duties**  
Cotton jeans face 16.6% duty plus 0.166% MPF fee. Synthetic blends get hit with 28.2%. To be fair, most first-time importers are shocked by this. Xintang Jeans provides landed cost estimates upfront so there are no surprises.

**Choosing the Wrong Shipping Incoterm**  
FOB is standard, but DDP (duty-paid) avoids surprises for first-time importers. Yes, it costs slightly more, but it eliminates the panic when customs sends you a bill you weren't expecting.

**Skipping Pre-Shipment Inspection**  
12% of bulk orders have defects if not checked. We recommend SGS or Bureau Veritas for orders over $5,000. That inspection fee pays for itself the first time it catches a problem.
