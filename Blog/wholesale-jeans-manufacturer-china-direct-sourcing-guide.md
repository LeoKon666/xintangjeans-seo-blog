## Verifying Authentic Manufacturers: 5 Red Flags Most Buyers Miss

### 1. Business License Mismatches

Check the Chinese AIC database to confirm registered capital exceeds ¥5 million RMB ($700k+), manufacturing scope is listed, and the address matches their facility photos. Trading companies typically show generic "import/export" licenses. This is your first filter.

### 2. Sample vs. Bulk Quality Gaps

Always request a pre-production sample (PPS) from the actual production line, not just their best work. Ask for random inspection reports from recent bulk orders and video of their washing equipment in operation. What we've seen repeatedly is that factories confident in their quality control won't hesitate to share this documentation.

### 3. Hidden MOQ Requirements

True factory MOQs by product type:

| Style            | Standard MOQ | Xintang Jeans MOQ |
|------------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Basic skinny     | 500+        | 200               |
| Distressed       | 300+        | 100               |
| Custom wash      | 1,000+      | 300               |
| Plus sizes       | 800+        | 150               |

Why does this matter? Because a trading company will promise lower MOQs, then consolidate your order with others and pocket the difference in unit cost.

### 4. Limited Customization

Real manufacturers offer private label tags ($0.15-$0.30/pc), custom hardware with buttons and rivets from $0.08/set, and exclusive washes with development fees between $150-$500. Trading companies claim they offer these services, but they're really just middlemen marking up factory pricing.

### 5. No Physical Address Verification

Insist on a live video tour of cutting and sewing lines, GPS coordinates of the washing facility, and the option for third-party audits. SGS or Bureau Veritas inspections run around $350. The factories that stand out are usually the ones that proactively suggest this step.

## Step-by-Step: How to Place Your First Wholesale Order

### 1. Select Your Product Tier

Budget tier ($6-$11 FOB) covers basic rigid or slight stretch with simple washes. Mid-range ($12-$19) gets you premium stretch denim and laser detailing. High-end ($20+) includes selvedge and hand-sanded whiskers. Start by determining which tier matches your target customer's expectations and your margin requirements.

### 2. Request Samples Properly

Email template to suppliers: "Please send 2 physical samples of your best-selling women's skinny jeans (size 28) in mid-blue wash. Include fabric swatches and specification sheet. Ready to pay sample fee plus shipping. Need production timeline for 300-piece order."

To be fair, this direct approach filters out non-serious suppliers immediately. Factories respond within 24-48 hours. Trading companies typically take 5-7 days.

### 3. Negotiate Payment Terms

Standard breakdown runs 30% deposit after sample approval and 70% balance before shipment. For new buyers, 50/50 splits risk more evenly. Some suppliers may resist, but any legitimate manufacturer understands this protects both parties on a first transaction.

### 4. Plan Logistics

Sea freight costs for 2026: 1 CBM holds approximately 300 pairs of jeans. Guangzhou to LA runs $120-$180/CBM. Door-to-door service adds $400-$600 for customs clearance. These numbers shift based on seasonal demand, so always request current quotes.

### 5. Quality Control Protocol

Xintang Jeans follows a 3-point check: fabric weight verification with +/- 5% tolerance, stitch density testing for 8-12 stitches per inch, and wash consistency verification across 10% of every order. This systematic approach catches issues before containers ship, not after they land in your warehouse.

## FAQ: China Jeans Sourcing Explained

**Q: How do I know if a supplier owns their factory?**  
Request their business license with manufacturing scope, then verify via the Chinese government site (zgcx.nacao.gov.cn). Real factories will show industrial park addresses, not commercial office buildings. Yes, this takes an extra 20 minutes, but it's saved countless buyers from multi-thousand-dollar mistakes.

**Q: What's the real minimum order quantity?**  
For standard styles in stock fabrics, 200 pieces is feasible. Custom developments require 300+ pieces to cover pattern and wash development costs. Anyone promising 50-piece MOQs on custom work is either lying about customization or padding prices to compensate.

**Q: How long does shipping take to the USA?**  
By sea, expect 22-28 days to the West Coast and 30-38 days to the East Coast. Air shipping takes 3-5 days but costs $4.50-$6.50/kg, making it practical only for urgent sample orders or high-margin rush shipments.

**Q: Can I get custom labels and packaging?**  
Yes, with MOQs of 300+ pieces for woven labels ($0.18/pc) and 500+ for printed hangtags ($0.12/pc). Xintang Jeans provides free design templates. Most suppliers charge $50-$150 for these templates, which tells you something about their volume and efficiency.
