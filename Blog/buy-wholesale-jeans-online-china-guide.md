## Step-by-Step Online Buying Process

### Step 1: Supplier Verification
Request three key documents before anything else. Business license (check registration with Chinese authorities). Factory photos with timestamps to verify production capacity exists. Recent third-party inspection reports - SGS or BV preferred, not their cousin's quality control company.

### Step 2: Sample Evaluation
Pay for 2-3 physical samples, which typically runs $50-$100 including shipping. Why does this matter? Because you're assessing stitch quality (12+ stitches per inch is industry standard), fabric weight for durability (10-14oz for wholesale jeans that'll last), and wash consistency since uneven fading kills reorders faster than anything.

### Step 3: Payment Terms Negotiation
Standard options break down like this: 30% deposit with 70% before shipment is most common for good reason. LC at sight offers more security but adds 2-3% in bank fees. PayPal works for orders under $3,000 but the fees hurt. For established relationships, some factories will negotiate better terms, but you'll need at least three successful orders first.

### Step 4: Production Monitoring
Require weekly photo updates during cutting and sewing phases. Pre-shipment inspection reports shouldn't be optional. Random QC checks on 10% of units catch problems before they become expensive disasters - we've seen this prevent six-figure losses more times than we can count.

### Step 5: Shipping Arrangements
Cost comparison for 500kg shipment to USA tells the real story:
- Sea freight: $800 (30-40 days)
- Air freight: $3,200 (5-7 days)  
- Express: $4,500 (3-5 days)

Most buyers choose sea freight for orders over 1,000 pieces, then switch to air for restocks when styles start selling faster than expected.

## Common Online Buying Mistakes

Not verifying fabric composition costs buyers thousands annually. Some suppliers substitute cotton with polyester blends, which your customers notice immediately. Always request lab test reports - yes, even from suppliers you've worked with before.

Accepting stock photos as quality proof? That's how 78% of wholesale jeans disputes start. The gap between expectations and reality widens dramatically when you skip physical samples.

To be fair, shipping costs surprise almost everyone initially. Sea freight charges can equal 15-20% of your product cost for smaller orders, which completely changes your margin calculations. Factor this in before you commit to pricing for your retail customers.

Skipping pre-shipment inspection saves $150 today. But that same inspection prevents an average of $2,100 in rejected shipments according to our tracking over the past three years. The math isn't complicated.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I avoid customs issues when importing jeans?**
A: Ensure your supplier provides three critical documents: commercial invoice with accurate HS code (6203.42 for denim trousers), packing list with net/gross weight, and Certificate of Origin for duty calculations. Missing any of these creates delays that can cost more than the duties themselves.

**Q: What's the real cost difference between Alibaba and direct suppliers?**
A: For 1,000 pairs of basic skinny jeans, you're looking at $9,500 through Alibaba ($9.50/pc) versus $6,800 direct from Xintang ($6.80/pc). That $2,700 difference pays for a lot of sampling and quality control, which is why experienced buyers eventually transition away from marketplaces.

**Q: How can I verify a supplier isn't just a trading company?**
A: Request factory floor videos with workers actually making jeans - not just warehouse tours. Ask for dye house and washing facility photos, since these require serious capital investment that trading companies don't make. Equipment lists showing industrial sewing machines should include brand names and quantities. Trading companies get vague here because they're coordinating multiple factories and can't provide specific details.
