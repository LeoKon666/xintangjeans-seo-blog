## Step-by-Step Factory Direct Order Process

### Step 1: Confirm Fabric Composition
Start here. 98% cotton/2% spandex is industry standard for shorts that hold their shape but still have give. If a supplier offers "8oz denim," walk away—proper shorts use 6-7oz fabric for drape and comfort. Heavier weight just doesn't work for this category.

### Step 2: Request Wash Samples
Factories keep what they call "wash boards" with 50+ finish options pinned to foam core. Some charge for these, some don't, but you need to see them before committing. Are you doing contrast stitching? Ask for Pantone-matched thread at this stage, not later when it's too late to adjust.

### Step 3: Negotiate Packing
Flat pack saves $0.30 per unit. Hanger pack adds $0.90 per unit. Our clients save $1,700 per container using vacuum bags instead of standard poly bags, and freight companies don't care as long as the cartons fit standard pallets. This is one of those areas where buyers leave money on the table without realizing it.

### Step 4: Schedule QC
Book inspections three days before shipment, not the day of. Standard defect allowance sits at 2.5% for shorts, but you want time to address issues before everything's sealed and loaded. Yes, this adds cost—usually $150-$300 depending on order size—but it's cheaper than discovering problems when your container arrives.

## 4 Costly Mistakes First-Time Buyers Make

**Skipping Pre-Production Samples**
What we've seen repeatedly: 68% of rushed orders have fit issues that could've been caught with a $35-50 sample. The waist sits wrong, the rise doesn't match spec sheets, the leg opening flares when it should taper. Pay for the sample.

**Ignoring Thread Color**
White thread on light denim disappears after washing and looks cheap. Specify "contrast gold" or "tone-on-tone" in your tech pack, not in a follow-up email three weeks into production when switching costs real money.

**Underestimating Trims**
Metal zippers add $0.40 versus nylon. Custom labels cost $0.15-$0.25 extra. Branded buttons run another $0.10-$0.15. These seem small until you multiply by 5,000 units and realize you've blown past your target cost by $3,500.

**Missing Shipping Windows**
September shipments face 2-3 week delays every single year because of Golden Week in China (October 1-7). Book vessel space by August 15th or you're rolling into October departures, which means November arrivals, which means you've missed early holiday shopping.

## FAQ

**Q: What's the minimum order for custom labels?**
A: 200 pieces for woven labels, 500 for embossed leather patches. Screen printing can be done at 100 pieces, but honestly, the setup cost per unit doesn't make sense unless you're confident in the style.

**Q: How do I compare fabric quality remotely?**
A: Request a "denim card"—factories will mail swatches with GSM and stretch tests documented. Some charge $20-30 for this service, some include it with serious inquiries. Either way, it's your only real option for evaluating hand feel before committing.

**Q: Can I mix styles in one order?**
