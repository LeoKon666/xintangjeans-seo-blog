## Step-by-Step: Importing Jeans from China to USA

### Step 1: Calculate Landed Costs

A $10 FOB pair becomes something very different after reality sets in. Ocean freight adds $1.20, customs duty (typically 10% on jeans) adds another $1.10, and trucking to your warehouse tacks on $0.80. Your true cost? $13.10 per unit. Too many new importers forget these hidden expenses and price themselves into losses.

### Step 2: Verify Supplier Credentials

Ask for their business license, export records (specifically past US shipment documentation), and quality control certificates. The factories that stand out are usually the ones who send these without being asked twice.

### Step 3: Order Samples

Expect to pay $25-$50 per sample style. Yes, the cost stings upfront. Ship via DHL or FedEx (3-5 days) instead of accepting "free shipping" that takes 30+ days — your time has value, and trend windows close fast.

### Step 4: Finalize Order Terms

Standard terms run 30% deposit with 70% due before shipment. Confirm production timelines upfront (typically 15-25 days for standard styles, longer for customization). Get it in writing. Verbal promises evaporate when production delays hit.

### Step 5: Arrange Logistics

Your freight forwarder should handle customs clearance while you focus on selling. CTPAT-certified carriers reduce inspection delays significantly — which matters when you're counting on inventory for a promotional weekend.
