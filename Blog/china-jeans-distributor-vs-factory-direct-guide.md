## What's the Difference? Distributors vs Factory Direct Explained

Jeans distributors act as middlemen between factories and international buyers. They consolidate products from multiple manufacturers, adding markup (typically 15-25%) while providing services like English communication, smaller MOQs (50-200 pieces vs 500+), mixed style orders, and quality inspection.

Factory-direct means buying straight from the manufacturer—like Xintang Jeans in China's denim capital. You trade middleman fees for lower FOB prices, customization control, direct quality oversight, and long-term production relationships. For buyers placing orders above 300 pieces who want control over design details, factory-direct consistently delivers better margins once you account for the full relationship value over multiple seasons.

## 5 Mistakes That Cost New Buyers Thousands

![denim quality control inspection process](unsplash:denim+quality+control+inspection+process)

**Assuming Alibaba sellers are factories:** Over 80% of "manufacturers" on B2B platforms are trading companies playing dress-up. Verify with factory floor videos, production capacity reports, and third-party audits like SGS. The factories that stand out are usually the ones sending you footage of specific equipment running your order, not generic workshop tours.

**Not testing fabric shrinkage:** Denim shrinks 3-8% after washing, which means your "perfect fit" samples become too-tight inventory nightmares. Always request pre-washed samples, wash test before bulk orders, and confirm shrinkage allowances are written into your purchase order.

**Ignoring payment terms:** Standard terms run 30% deposit, 70% before shipment. Avoid suppliers demanding 100% upfront—that's the clearest red flag in wholesale sourcing. Yes, the deposit protects the factory's production costs, but you need leverage too.

**Skipping pre-shipment inspection:** Professional QC catches stitching defects (12% of rejected shipments), size discrepancies (23% of claims), and color variations (9% of issues). Spending $200-400 on inspection prevents $5,000-15,000 losses from defective containers you can't return economically.

**Underestimating shipping costs:** Recent examples show 500kg to USA costs $1,200-$1,800, while 1,000kg to EU runs $2,400-$3,600. Always get FOB and CIF quotes—what looks like amazing unit pricing becomes mediocre once you add freight that doubles your landed cost per piece.

## When to Choose a Distributor (And When Not To)

### Best Cases for Distributors

For testing new styles, distributors let you order 50-100 pieces across multiple designs without committing to production runs. Small boutiques with under $5,000 initial budgets benefit from the flexibility despite higher per-unit costs. Urgent replenishment needs also favor distributors—2-3 week lead times beat factory-direct's 6-8 weeks when you're out of your bestselling wash.

### When Factory-Direct Pays Off

Scaling proven styles sees unit costs drop 18-25% at 500+ pieces, which matters enormously when you're reordering twice per season. Custom designs give you control over every detail from pocket shape to wash formulas—the stuff that differentiates your brand from everyone else buying the same distributor stock. Quality-sensitive buyers gain direct access to production floor inspections instead of trusting middleman reports.

Why does this matter? Because distributors optimize for transaction volume while factories optimize for manufacturing excellence. Your priorities determine which matters more.

## FAQ: China Jeans Sourcing Questions Answered

**Q: How do I verify a real jeans factory?**  
Request factory registration documents, production line videos, and references from past international buyers. Xintang Jeans provides all three upfront because we've learned that transparency during the inquiry phase predicts smoother production relationships later.

**Q: What's the real cost difference factory vs distributor?**  
On 500+ piece orders, factory-direct averages 22-38% cheaper after accounting for all fees. The savings increase with order size—at 1,000+ pieces, factory-direct often beats distributors by 40-50% while delivering better customization.

**Q: Can I get samples before ordering?**  
Yes. Reputable suppliers provide samples for $25-50, often credited against future orders. If a factory refuses samples or quotes $200+ for basic styles, they're either not serious manufacturers or testing whether you're a serious buyer.

**Q: How long do shipments take?**  
Typical timelines run production at 4-6 weeks plus shipping at 2-3 weeks by air or 4-5 weeks by sea. Rush production exists (2-3 weeks) but costs 15-25% extra and requires confirming availability before you count on it for launch deadlines.
