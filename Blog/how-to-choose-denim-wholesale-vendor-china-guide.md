## Step-by-Step Vendor Evaluation Process

### Step 1: Verify Physical Factory Existence

Request a video walkthrough showing production lines, not just the showroom. Check that the business license matches the factory address. Confirm DUNS number or other international registration.

What we've seen repeatedly is that many "factories" on B2B sites are actually trading companies. We always share our Xintang factory GPS coordinates with buyers.

### Step 2: Assess Denim-Specific Capabilities

A true denim specialist will have in-house washing facilities (not outsourced), sample rooms with 100+ fabric swatches, CAD pattern-making systems, and quality control stations every 5 workers.

### Step 3: Test Communication Responsiveness

Send 3 emails at different times: a technical question about stitch types, a pricing request for small MOQ, and a problem scenario like "What if colors don't match?"

Delayed responses or boilerplate answers? That signals future issues.

### Step 4: Validate Quality Control Systems

Ask for QC checklists used at each stage. Request photos of failed items from their last shipment. Get third-party lab test reports from SGS or ITS.

### Step 5: Order Pre-Production Samples

Never judge by stock samples. Pay for 2 pairs in different sizes, your exact fabric and wash specification, and standard lead time—not rushed.

## Common Mistakes When Choosing Vendors

For most brands, the biggest trap is prioritizing price over capability. Low quotes often mean subcontracting to smaller workshops without proper quality systems. Skipping the video audit? Photos can be staged, but live video reveals actual working conditions and equipment.

To be fair, many buyers discover too late that their "24/7 support" only operates 9-5 China time. Testing communication upfront prevents these headaches.

Some factories maintain certifications for show but don't follow the processes daily. Yes, the payment terms matter, but demanding 50%+ upfront may indicate cash flow problems (30% deposit is standard).
