## 6 Wholesale Jeans Types Every Boutique Should Stock

### 1. Vintage Washed Straight Leg (Best Seller)
This style accounts for 58% of top-moving inventory in boutiques we work with. Start with 50 pieces in 3 core sizes. Ask Xintang factories for "enzyme wash" to get that authentic vintage look without the stiff hand feel that cheaper processes produce.

### 2. Plus Size Stretch Skinny
Here's what most boutiques miss: 67% of plus size shoppers struggle to find quality denim that fits. MOQ is usually 30 pieces minimum due to specialized patterns. Watch the fabric recovery closely—cheap stretch denim bags out after a few wears, which means returns and damaged reputation.

### 3. Raw Selvedge Denim (Premium)
This commands 2-3x retail markup. Yes, the MOQ is higher—typically 100+ pieces because of fabric minimums—but the margins justify it. Look for 12oz+ Japanese or Turkish denim. Anything lighter won't hold the structure your customers expect at premium price points.

## The Boutique's Step-by-Step Sourcing Process

### Step 1: Define Your Price Anchor
Calculate your target retail price first, then work backward. For a $120 retail jean, you need $40 landed cost, $20 for shipping and duties, leaving $60 gross profit. Most boutiques get this backward and end up either overpriced or underwater on margins.

### Step 2: Request Swatch Books
Reputable suppliers provide physical denim samples before you commit to anything. Test three things: colorfastness (rub with white cloth), stretch recovery (pull and release), and weight (12-14oz is ideal for year-round wear). If a supplier resists sending swatches, that tells you everything.

### Step 3: Order Proto Samples
Pay the $50-100 sample fee. It's worth every dollar to test fit on actual customers, wash consistency, and stitching quality. The factories that stand out are usually the ones who already have refined their sample process because they know it closes deals.

### Step 4: Negotiate MOQs
Most Xintang factories accept 30-50pcs for standard styles, 100pcs for custom washes, and 500pcs for full private label. To be fair, they prefer higher quantities, but competition has made them more flexible for boutiques that show repeat order potential.

## 5 Costly Mistakes Boutique Owners Make

Not testing shrinkage is the number one problem. A size 32 that becomes 30 after washing means returns, and returns kill your margins faster than anything else. We've also seen boutiques ignore fabric composition entirely—polyester-blend denim pills after 10 wears, which your customers will remember.

Skipping pre-shipment inspection? Roughly 12% of first orders have quality issues that could've been caught. Yes, the MOQ matters, but choosing price over reliability often costs more long-term when you factor in defects, delays, and damaged relationships with your customers. And don't underestimate duties—US denim tariffs average 16.5%, which changes your entire cost structure if you forgot to include it.

## Boutique Owner FAQ

**Q: How do I know the quality before ordering?**
Always start with swatches and paid samples. We provide video of actual production batches before shipment, not stock footage. Most suppliers won't do this because their quality varies batch to batch.

**Q: What's the real lead time including shipping?**
15 days production plus 10-12 days sea freight to US West Coast. Air shipping is available for rush orders but adds about $5/kg, which can double your freight cost on heavy denim.

**Q: Can I mix styles to meet MOQ?**
Yes, and honestly this is the smartest approach for new boutiques. Combine 30pcs of 3 styles to reach 90pcs minimum. Why does this matter? Because testing multiple styles gives you real sales data before you commit to reorders.
